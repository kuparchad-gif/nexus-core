# Micro Entity Qualification Guide for Patent Filing

## What is Micro Entity Status?

Micro Entity status provides a 75% reduction in USPTO patent fees. For example:
- **Regular Fee**: $1,600 basic filing fee
- **Small Entity Fee**: $800 (50% reduction)
- **Micro Entity Fee**: $400 (75% reduction)

## Qualification Requirements

To qualify as a Micro Entity, you must meet ALL of the following criteria:

### 1. Small Entity Qualification
You must first qualify as a small entity, meaning:
- **Individual inventor**, OR
- **Small business** with fewer than 500 employees, OR
- **Nonprofit organization**

### 2. Gross Income Limit
Your gross income in the previous calendar year must not exceed **3 times the median household income** for the United States.

**2023 Median Household Income**: ~$70,000
**2023 Micro Entity Threshold**: ~$210,000 gross income

### 3. Patent Application Limit
You must not have been named as an inventor on more than **4 previously filed patent applications** (excluding provisional applications that were never converted).

### 4. Assignment Restrictions
You must not have assigned, granted, or conveyed (and not be under obligation to do so) a license or other ownership interest in the application to an entity that does not qualify as a small entity.

## Required Documentation

### For Micro Entity Status:
1. **IRS Form 1040** (most recent tax return showing gross income)
2. **Micro Entity Certification Form** (USPTO Form PTO/SB/15A)
3. **Declaration of Patent Application History**

### For Small Business Qualification:
If claiming small business status, you may need:
1. **Business registration documents**
2. **Employee count verification**
3. **Revenue documentation**

## How to Prove Micro Business Status

### Option 1: Individual Inventor
- Simply file as an individual
- Provide personal tax returns
- No business documentation needed

### Option 2: Micro Business
If you have a business entity:
1. **Register your business** (LLC, Corporation, etc.)
2. **Maintain business records** showing:
   - Fewer than 500 employees
   - Gross income below threshold
   - Business activities related to the invention
3. **File business tax returns** (Form 1120, 1120S, or 1065)

## Filing Process

### Step 1: Prepare Documentation
- Gather tax returns
- Complete micro entity forms
- Prepare patent application

### Step 2: File Provisional Application
- **Cost**: $320 (micro entity fee)
- **Protection**: 1 year
- **Benefit**: Establishes priority date

### Step 3: File Non-Provisional (within 12 months)
- **Cost**: ~$760 total fees (micro entity)
- **Protection**: Full patent examination
- **Timeline**: 2-4 years to grant

## Cost Breakdown (Micro Entity)

### Provisional Application:
- **Basic Filing Fee**: $320
- **Total**: $320

### Non-Provisional Application:
- **Basic Filing Fee**: $400
- **Search Fee**: $160
- **Examination Fee**: $200
- **Total**: $760

### Additional Possible Fees:
- **Excess Claims**: $20 per claim over 20
- **Multiple Dependent Claims**: $100
- **Patent Issue Fee**: $240 (if patent grants)

## Important Notes

### Maintain Micro Entity Status
- Must re-certify micro entity status for each fee payment
- If income exceeds threshold, must pay higher fees
- Cannot retroactively claim micro entity status

### Business Formation Considerations
- You can form a business and still qualify as micro entity
- Business must also meet small entity requirements
- Individual filing is often simpler for solo inventors

### Professional Help
Consider hiring a patent attorney for:
- **Prior art search**: $1,000-$3,000
- **Patent drafting**: $5,000-$15,000
- **Prosecution**: $2,000-$5,000
